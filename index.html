conceptual framework

i wanted to explore different expressions of of modern pop cultures through a variety of household pets. the consolidation of popular media and pets represents three forms of globalization: trade, textile exchange, and the internet. prior to the internet, diversity in culture, ideas, and expression were limited to the real and tangible, especially inaccessible to the average. the internet facilitated not only an explosion of cultural exchange and ideas, but also the ability to make the intangible tangible, or in other words, make the inexperienced able to be experienced. no longer limited by the physical, the unreal begins to blur with the real. this manifests itself literally with online shopping and global shipping, but also visually, with videos, images, and websites, which are only exacerbated with the onset of editing softwares. instead of needing the real-life to experience, simulation serves as the tangible and experiencable, providing another platform for human connection. the personification and projection upon household pets becomes accessible as a medium for human connection through photoshop and the internet: the non-human made human.

i intend to explore this concept through different fashion styles imposed upon pets, as well as the superimposement of household pets in human activities. i seek to convey a fundamental theme of human connection and joy through shared relatability with pop culture, whether it be in the form of fashion, memes, or food, using household pets as a medium for all to enjoy. moreso, it is my goal to show human universalism using pets, which in of themselves are an example of a universal love

conceptual framework

Conceptual Framework I wanted to explore different expressions of of modern pop cultures through a variety of household pets. The consolidation of popular media and pets represents three forms of globalization: trade, textile exchange, and the Internet. Prior to the Internet, diversity in culture, ideas, and expression were limited to the real and tangible, especially inaccessible to the average. The Internet facilitated not only an explosion of cultural exchange and ideas, but also the ability to make the intangible tangible, or in other words, make the inexperienced able to be experienced. No longer limited by the physical, the unreal begins to blur with the real. This manifests itself literally with online shopping and global shipping, but also visually, with videos, images, and websites, which are only exacerbated with the onset of editing softwares. Instead of needing the real-life to experience, simulation serves as the tangible and experiencable, providing another platform for human connection. The personification and projection upon household pets becomes accessible as a medium for human connection through Photoshop and the Internet: the non-human made human. I intend to explore this concept through different fashion styles imposed upon pets, as well as the superimposement of household pets in human activities. I seek to convey a fundamental theme of human connection and joy through shared relatability with pop culture, whether it be in the form of fashion, memes, or food, using household pets as a medium for all to enjoy.

final submission

my American cat is pictured with a Mexican tequila and French herbal liquor, and dances in a local Los Angeles club. she dons a mustard-yellow Minions cap--invented in France, franchised in the US, manufactured in China. the music is electronic and fast-paced, featuring layered thumping drumlines and synths--simply electric! the songs featured at the club are drum n bass and breakcore, both originating from working class Jamaican immigrants in the UK.

in real life, Juno has an affinity for silver vine and catnip, which flings her into a fit of spinning, kicking, and rolling... not too different from your local music show. inspired by her proclivities for stimulants, i inserted her into an electronic dance club with a buzzing, eccentric crowd.
